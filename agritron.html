Description

Agritron is an eight-wheeled tank-drive robot built to autonomously harvest plants in the field. It drives down crop rows on its own and cuts each plant using blades mounted at the end of a three-degree-of-freedom arm, combining navigation, arm kinematics, and end-effector design into one platform.

I designed and prototyped the mechanical systems and robotic assemblies, handling CAD modelling, fabrication, and iterative testing as the MVP came together. The eight-wheel drivetrain was chosen for traction and stability over uneven ground, where a lighter platform would struggle.
