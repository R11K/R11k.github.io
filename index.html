Sheet 01 — About Me

From a garage full of tools to farm robotics

I grew up in York Region, north of Toronto, where two things shaped me: easy access to the outdoors, which made hiking a lifelong way to find peace in a chaotic world, and a garage with enough room to fill with tools and half-finished projects. My mother started her own STEM education business when I was ten because she believed hands-on learning should be accessible — and she never leaves things half-done. From her I learned that persistence and finishing what you start is a skill.

For a long time I assumed my future was in computer science because I loved video games. Then I joined my high school’s FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC) team, and everything shifted. I discovered the magic of taking an idea from a screen and making it real. The first time I drove the robot we had built settled the question, and I changed direction toward mechatronics.

That’s why I chose York and the Lassonde School of Engineering — an exceptional machine shop and hands-on labs. Because I learn best by doing, a school that lets me build rather than only study is exactly where I belong.
